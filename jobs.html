But first, let’s start with the Burning Glass Technology (BGT) IT Industry data.

What is BGT Data

The University of Adelaide describes BGT as data obtained and analysed from millions of job listings and resumes via usage of an artificial intelligence application to determine several key findings, such as what skillsets are most sought after by employers and how these skillsets vary between each industry. (University of Adelaide, 2020). The data obtained via BGT will “identify three different skill types, namely: Baseline Skills, Technical Skills and Software Skills.” (University of Adelaide, 2020).

After speaking directly with both pharmacists and a local Doctor, I have found that both would appreciate and benefit from such a system. Put simply, the benefit of this application would be to securely have a direct line of communication between doctors, pharmacists, and other healthcare professionals to avoid any errors. We hope this app will help with common errors that occur due to today's lack of professional communication. Common errors included: Duplication of therapy (either getting two of the same medication or two drugs in the same class). Omitted but necessary medications. Unreported use of herbals or dietary supplements TheConversation.com (2018). Common mistakes on prescriptions and issues with transferring of care can take up to 48 hours to resolve using today’s industry standard of communication between professionals.

To quickly summarise what these mean in the scheme of employment opportunity and Information Technology based careers:

1. Baseline Skills: Outlines skills that are relevant to the job that are not job specific. For example, this tends to focus on skillsets that a potential employee may have such as communication skills, problem solving and organisational skills (BGT, 2019:4) which have context that can be applied to almost any job.

2. Technical Skills: These are skills that outline desired expertise and understanding required to complete certain technical tasks, such as that of having experience with programming, data analysis and server maintenance. (BGT, 2019:4)

3. Software Skills: These are skillsets that outline desired experience in various computer software to complete the job effectively, which may include expertise in SQL, Adobe CS and SAP. (BGT, 2019:4)

Within the BGT data, we can see immediately (with each page listed in order, respectively) (BGT, 2018):

1. What baseline skills are most sought after by employers,

2. What specialised skills are most sought after by employers (mix of technical and software skills),

3. A list of which jobs are considered the top IT titles,

4. The experience of how many years worked within the industry required to be considered for the role, and;

5. A list of top IT Occupations.
